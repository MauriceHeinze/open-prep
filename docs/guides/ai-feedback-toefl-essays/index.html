How to Get AI Feedback on TOEFL® Essays

AI can be a powerful TOEFL® Writing coach — but only if the feedback is structured and actionable. Learn how to move from vague chatbot replies to feedback that actually improves your score.

Why most AI feedback is too vague

When you paste an essay into a general AI chatbot, you usually get broad comments like “good job” or “improve grammar.” That is not enough to study from. TOEFL® raters look for specific skills: task response, organization, development, language use, and mechanics. Your feedback should map to those same categories.

What useful AI feedback looks like

Useful AI feedback on a TOEFL® essay should include:

  • An overall score — ideally on the TOEFL® 0–30 scale or CEFR level.
  • Criterion subscores — breakdowns for task response, organization, development, and language use.
  • Specific improvement points — concrete weaknesses with examples from your essay.
  • Phrase-level suggestions — highlighted words or sentences with more academic alternatives.
  • Actionable next steps — what to focus on in your next attempt.

How to prompt an AI for better TOEFL® feedback

If you are using ChatGPT, Claude, or another general model, include these instructions in your prompt:

  1. Ask for a TOEFL® Writing score on the 0–30 scale.
  2. Request subscores for task response, organization, development, and language use.
  3. Ask for three specific strengths and three specific weaknesses.
  4. Request rewritten sentences for weak phrases.
  5. Tell the AI to base feedback on the official TOEFL® Writing rubrics.

How to study from AI feedback

  • Track recurring weaknesses. If organization is always low, practice outlining before writing.
  • Rewrite, don’t just read. Take one weak sentence and rewrite it using the AI’s suggestion.
  • Compare attempts. Save essays and look for score trends over time.
  • Focus on one skill per session. Don’t try to fix grammar, organization, and vocabulary all at once.
