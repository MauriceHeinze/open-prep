# Video Editor Brief

## Project Summary

`open-prep` is an open source, local-first macOS desktop app for standardized English exam preparation.

The first release focuses on TOEFL®-style writing practice. A learner picks a prompt, writes an essay, submits it, and receives AI-assisted rubric feedback with scores, explanations, and targeted improvement notes.

The app is designed for people who already have a local AI CLI installed. It works with either ChatGPT Plus users who have Codex installed locally or users who have Claude Code installed locally.

The broader product direction is a calm, private, academically credible prep platform for TOEFL®, IELTS, and Cambridge across writing, speaking, reading, and listening.

## What The Video Should Communicate

- This is an open source project
- This is a serious study tool, not a generic chatbot
- The app is focused, private, and local-first
- The AI workflow depends on a locally installed Codex or Claude Code CLI
- The workflow is simple: choose prompt, write, submit, review feedback
- The value is structured evaluation, clarity, and progress

## Tone And Pacing

- Calm and confident
- Clean and readable
- Professional rather than playful
- Modern, but not flashy
- Fast enough to feel efficient, but not rushed

## Visual Style

- Use a mostly light, neutral interface
- Keep layouts spacious and restrained
- Let the content feel academic and trustworthy
- Use color sparingly for emphasis, buttons, focus, and feedback
- Avoid loud ed-tech visuals, cartoon cues, or overly playful motion
- Avoid generic AI imagery like chat bubbles, glowing robots, or obvious sci-fi effects

## Suggested Edit Flow

1. Open with the app in a calm desktop environment
2. Show prompt selection to establish the writing workflow
3. Show essay entry and submission
4. Transition into the evaluation state
5. Highlight structured feedback, scores, and annotated improvements
6. End on the idea of focused practice and measurable progress

## Exact Brand Colors

- Primary accent: `#c6005c`
- App background: `#f8f8f8`
- White surface/card: `#ffffff`
- Main text: `#131313`
- Secondary surface: `#f4f4f5`
- Muted fill: `#f1f3f3`
- Borders/dividers: `#e3e7e8`
- Subdued text: `#67787c`
- Success: `#149545`
- Warning: `#f5a000`
- Error/destructive: `#e7000b`

## Fonts Used
- Nunito for headings
- Roboto Sans for everything else

## Color Usage Guidance

- Use `#c6005c` for primary actions, active states, focus accents, and key brand moments
- Keep backgrounds, panels, and cards mostly white or very light gray
- Use the green, amber, and red only for status and feedback
- Do not reintroduce the old purple/green brand palette

## Do Not

- Do not make the video feel like a social app
- Do not make it feel like a cloud-first AI assistant demo
- Do not overuse animation or motion graphics
- Do not use heavy gradients or neon styling
- Do not use childish school icons or generic graduation imagery
